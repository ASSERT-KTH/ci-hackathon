ABOUT


This is a contribution to the KTH CI hackathon, the first ever CI art hackathon to take place. Our goal with this project is to show how the internet is alive and constantly pulsing with activity. We have chosen to visually represent this by a pulsing nebula where the size of each 'beat' is dependent on the amount of successful commits travis has registered since last 'beat'. In the background you hear the sound of a heartbeat that is connected to the pulse of the nebula

TRAVIS CI


For this hackathon, we focus on one CI engine: Travis CI. We have selected it because it helps the developers of thousands of open source projects to make sure that the changes they contribute are correct. Travis CI is literally the factory where the key components of the modern web and mobile applications are built.

REAKTORHALLEN


The hackathon will take place in reaktorhallen, a.k.a R1. This is a very special place, 25 meters underground, where Swedish researchers had an experimental nuclear reactor and investigated nuclear physics from 1954 to 1970. In 1982, the reactor was completetely dismantled. Since 1998, the place is managed by KTH and used mainly for creative events, art exhibitions and installation, seminars and movie production.
